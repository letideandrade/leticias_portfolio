    Explore my Branding Design & Graphic Design projects!

    The Branding Design projects in this section involved crafting visual identities that represent a company or individual, encompassing logos, color schemes, typography, and overall brand aesthetics. The graphic design projects in this section encompass a wide range of visual communication tasks, including creating posters, advertisements, digital illustrations, and packaging designs, aimed at conveying messages effectively through visual elements and enhancing user experience.

  • Priscila's Bakery - Branding Design

    March 2023

    Priscila's Bakery - Branding Design Project This project was very special to me. A childhood friend of mine, who is highly talented in the baking/pastry field, decided to open her own business. She needed a brand to represent her business and visual identity for her new venture, well as a website to showcase her work and a modile App for customers to browse products and place orders. I was brainstorming for the brand, slogan and logo ideas until she chose this one logo that I had initially sketched by hand on a piece of paper. Then, I recreated the chosen logo using Adobe Illustrator to create a vector graphic, so she could use it for printing purposes in her physical store, on uniforms, and in pamphlets.

  • Import Store - Ideation Process

  • Board Game Project - Part I

    June 2018

    Board Game Project - Part I: Ideation and Design The purpose of this project was to develop a game that encourages socialization and learning among children aged 10 to 12. This board game serves as a supplementary educational tool for English classes, designed to engage both children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and neurotypical children. The goal is to promote inclusive socialization and learning in schools. The game's visual interface was created to be accessible to all students, and the prototype was tested in classes at Yazigi School (an ESL school in Brazil), where both autistic and neurotypical students learn together. The game enhances traditional educational materials by making lessons more enjoyable and interactive. The project aims to benefit both children with special needs and regular children by fostering engagement, promoting socialization, and enhancing knowledge.

  • Board Game Project - Part II

    July 2018

    Board Game Project - Part II: Testing The second part of this project involved developing the board game prototype for testing and obtaining feedback from players to address possible errors and improve playability before finalizing the project. The prototype was printed on canvas, and the cards were made of 300 gsm paper. The game was tested with four groups of students at Yazigi School. Feedback indicated that all students, including those with special needs (as this project aims to include), were able to play easily, without doubts concerning the game's mechanics. The visual interface was designed to be easily understood by both autistic and neurotypical children, promoting interaction and learning between them. The feedback also confirmed that the game was accessible and effective in fostering interaction and learning, serving as a useful and enjoyable tool to promote knowledge in schools.
